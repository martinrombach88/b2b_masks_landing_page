The Effect of Mask Wearing on SARS-CoV-2 Infection Risk

Study Location: Canada
Source: Karaivanov et al
Population studied: Country population
Intervention: Mandatory mask wearing indoors
Outcome: Decline in new diagnoses by 25-40% every week
Study Location: Boston, Massachusetts, USA
Source: Wang X et al
Population studied: 9850 Health care workers
Intervention: Universal masking of health care workers and patients in the Mass General Brigham hospital
Outcome: Decline of new diagnoses among health care workers by 3.4% every week
Study Location: Jena, Germany
Source: Mitze et al
Population studied: City population aged 15+
Intervention: Mandatory mask wearing in public spaces (e.g. public transport, shops)
Outcome: Decline of new diagnoses among population by 1.32% every day
Study Location: Households in Beijing, China
Source: Wang Y et al
Population studied: 124 Households of diagnosed cases comprising 335 people
Intervention: Self reported mask wearing by victim/victim's family member prior to infection
Outcome: Mask wearing reduced risk of secondary infection by 79%
Study Location: Bangkok, Thailand
Source: Doung-ngern et al
Population studied: 839 Close contacts of 211 index cases
Intervention: Self reported mask wearing by contact at time of high-risk exposure to case
Outcome: Always having used a mask reduced infection risk by 77%
Study Location: USS Theodore Roosevelt, Guam
Source: Payne et al
Population studied: 383 US Navy service members
Intervention: Self-reported mask wearing
Outcome: Mask wearing reduced risk of infection by 70%
